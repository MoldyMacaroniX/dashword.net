The Geometry Dash fanbase reacted with heavy backlash after [YouTuber Waboo](/posts/growth-is-not-an-excuse-for-bad-videos/) uploaded a showcase of an eight minute long extension to the popular level "Skeletal Shenanigans" he calls "Skeletal Extremigans."

The level contains many advertisements for "Skeletal Plushies," a product Waboo is using this level to promote. But despite being based on and marketed using the work of Yoreid and Airz—the two original creators of Skeletal Shenanigans—Waboo did not get permission from them to monetize their work.

Waboo claims Skeletal Extremigans and the plushie business associated with it cost him a total of $115,000 to create, a staggering figure that no doubt attracts eyes to this level and his plushie business.

# Yoreid's Response

Yoreid, one of the creators of Skeletan Shenanigans, [gave this statement](https://www.youtube.com/post/Ugkx_gkaZT46A1PSanIoRoBhfcwvnUiXfmfI) in response to Skeletan Extremigans. He says he didn't want to get involved but now feels "trapped."

He says his inaction led to others assuming he was involved and thereby hurting his reputation:



> People are seeing the budget for this (a lot of which is just the plushies, which I didn't shut down either because again I didn't care and didn't want to be involved) and are immediately lashing out and calling it corporate slop. And honestly, I understand some amount, I don't think levels should be built under this motive at all, but because of my inaction everyone is assuming I was fully involved and now it seems like my reputation is going to take a hit for literally no reason.

Skeletal Shenanigans has become a level much more popular than even Yoreid and Airz thought possible. As such, it has been in lots of controversies, such as when it was blamed for stealing the [Geometry Dash Awards](/posts/geometry-dash-awards-explained-what-are-they/):

> I was blamed for stealing the gd awards from the other nominees. And now I'm going to be blamed for the extension. I am just so unbelievably tired.

Overall, Yoreid says he doesn't want any of this and just wants to be proud of his work:

> I care way too much about my public image and I hate it so much. I made something I should just be proud of and nothing else but instead it has this never-ending nightmare of a tumor attached to it that just keeps growing.

We recommend reading [Yoreid's full statement on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/post/Ugkx_gkaZT46A1PSanIoRoBhfcwvnUiXfmfI).

Despite the fact that Waboo's plushie business is arguably copyright infringement, [he has shown no plans to stop what he's doing](https://x.com/motleyorc/status/2040079359923437838).

It is also worth noting that all this negative publicity is still publicity and may end up helping Waboo. If you don't want to support this, the best thing to do is simply ignore it rather than engage with it.
