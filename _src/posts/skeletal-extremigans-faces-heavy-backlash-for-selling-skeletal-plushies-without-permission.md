The Geometry Dash fanbase reacted with heavy backlash after YouTuber Waboo uploaded a showcase of an eight minute long extension to the popular level "Skeletal Shenanigans" he calls "Skeletal Extremigans."

The level contains many advertisements for "Skeletal Plushies," a product Waboo is using this level to promote. But despite being based on and marketed using the work of Yoreid and Airz—the two original creators of Skeletal Shenanigans—Waboo did not get permission from them to monetize their work.

Waboo claims Skeletal Extremigans and the plushie business associated with it cost him a total of $115,000 to create, a staggering figure that no doubt attracts eyes to this level and his plushie business.
Yoreid, one of the creators of Skeletan Shenanigans, gave this statement in response to Skeletan Extremigans:

> I actually feel so trapped rn
> 

> A few months ago now I heard about Waboo's extension of sksh and the $7k budget behind it and I thought "oh, wild" and then moved on with my life, didn't address it or ask any further because I didn't care and frankly even if I did it wasn't my place to go and tell someone "hey you can't make an extension of my level, I own all skeleton themed levels."
> 

> Then the number turned into $20k, then it turned into $100k, and now the showcase is fast approaching. First of all, from a creator's perspective, this is honestly just a cool thing I'll be able to see as an unofficial successor to some project I made last year. But from the perspective of what has happened from the community's eyes, I actually feel like I'm screwed. 

> People are seeing the budget for this (a lot of which is just the plushies, which I didn't shut down either because again I didn't care and didn't want to be involved) and are immediately lashing out and calling it corporate slop. And honestly, I understand some amount, I don't think levels should be built under this motive at all, but because of my inaction everyone is assuming I was fully involved and now it seems like my reputation is going to take a hit for literally no reason.
> 

> I feel like this has been happening consistently for nearly a year now and I don't understand it. Literally all I did is upload a level onto the gd servers, that's it. Then it was just a matter of me being indirectly blamed for things I didn't do. I was blamed for lag and bugs because it being the event level prevented me from updating it. I was blamed for it being the event level for like 3 months with no change. I was blamed for making an 'overrated' level despite me having no control over its virality. I was blamed for making a content farm. I was blamed for stealing the gd awards from the other nominees. And now I'm going to be blamed for the extension. I am just so unbelievably tired. 
> 

> And even venting about this feels terrible, because I know for a fact that despite this extension being made ultimately with a monetary incentive, there are real people behind the creation of this extension who really are just people who like sksh and tried their best to make something worthy of succeeding it. So complaining about this just makes me feel like I'm shifting blame to them which I don't want to do because I never shut down this project myself, which again I didn't want to do because that itself would've made me feel like an uptight loser.
> 

> I care way too much about my public image and I hate it so much. I made something I should just be proud of and nothing else but instead it has this never-ending nightmare of a tumor attached to it that just keeps growing.
