Since launched in 2022, community contributions have been at our core. Both our [codebase](https://github.com/MoldyMacaroniX/dashword.net) and [articles](/posts/) feature contributions from [our wonderful contributors](/authors/).

Since community contributions are important to us, we want to be fully transparent regarding our ownership, monetization, and editorial independence.

# Ownership

- The Dashword.net domain, website, and branding is owned by [Moldy](https://www.moldygd.com/)
- All code is licensed under the [MIT license](https://github.com/MoldyMacaroniX/dashword.net?tab=License-1-ov-file)
- All articles are owned by their respective authors, as per our [Author Terms](/author-terms/)

# Monetization

We show ads on the Dashword.net website. Currently, all ad revenue goes to Moldy. This is because Moldy owns and operates the Dashword.net website and therefore is responsible for all its expenses.

Right now, authors do not get paid for articles they contribute to Dashword.net. In the future, we might look into creating a revenue sharing program. But as of now, no such program exists.

We believe this is fair. We host your content for you and you still control the rights to it. If you ever want it off our site, all you have to do is let us know.

# Editorial Independence

In order to report news fairly, we occasionally publish articles that are critical of people and groups, such as by reporting on allegations made by others against these people and groups, or by coming up with our own critiques in our [opinion](/categories/opinion/) category.

This means that, sometimes, we may publish articles that we do not agree with or be forced to report on allegations that we do not agree with. **And we believe this is our greatest strength—our commitment to free speech.**

We also make an effort to eliminate conflicts of interest. As such, we do not allow authors to cover people or groups where they have a personal conflict unless their coverage is strictly uncontroversial (such as writing about a project they started). For example, authors are not allowed to report on allegations made about themselves. However, as of May 4, 2026, we have a strict **Right To Reply** policy to allow them to respond and be featured in any articles discussing them in a potentially bad light. In specific:

- Any article covering someone or some group in a potentially negative light is required to reach out to the person/group in question to give them the opportunity to respond and have their response published in the same article critiquing them.
- The person/group in question must be reached out to privately (such as through email or private messages on social media).
- If it is impossible to reach them privately, posting a public message (such as a tweet) directed towards them is allowed, but it must only be a request for them to reach out privately in order to not leak the contents of the author's investigation.
- If the person/group in question does not respond within 24 hours of the message being sent, the article will be published without their comment. That being said, if they do later reply, we will still add it to the article.
- The responsibility to reach out to the people/groups in question rests in the author of the article critiquing them, although Dashword.net may assist them.
